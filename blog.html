Flowering Season & Markets of India

Speaking in flowers has been the most enchanting way to get on the horn for ages. Forevermore, flowers and weddings have been in a relationship of long standing. In a country like India, most of the people accentuate on flower decorations while planning out their weddings. Right from the wedding bouquet of a bride, the floral downpour by bridesmaids, the boutonnieres of the groom and groomsmen, to the blossoming aisles and reception tables; flowers have an integral roleplay in our weddings. However, now-a-days it’s just substantially more! While giving a thought to the contemporary floral-wedding elements, customized flower jewelry and hair accessories are the cornerstones of a bridal apparel. The floral cascades dripping with flowers are one of the legitimate beauties in the recent wedding trends. Moreover, blooming backdrop for the bride and groom is yet another embracing plug-in for a perfect fairly-tale wedding album!

The October-February time frame that witness the winter season in India is the most accessible time for different species of beautiful blooming annuals. The seeds of winter flowering annuals are sown in October-November that almost hibernate during the cold months of December or January and start growing as soon as the spring sets in. Hence, the winter weddings can have the advantage of these seasonal flowers having a tremendous variety of colour, size and form in an affordable budget. However, in order to incorporate a galore of flowers in your wedding episode, sky isn’t the limit! It’s your budget allocation, flowering seasons, and above all the accessibility to the top flower markets of India.

Here is the list of the top-notch flower markets explored by The Planning Paradise just for you!

Krishna Rajendra (KR) Market, Bangalore –

The Krishna Rajendra (KR) Market in Bangalore (also known as City Market) undertakes most of the wholesale trade in traditional flowers. Modern flowers are traded in Russel market. Jasmine, Crossandra, Marigold, Chrysanthemums and Aster are the major flower species traded here. However, insignificant quantities of Rose, both in loose blooms or long stemmed and Gladiolus are also traded. This market witnesses a seasonal arrival of flowers. Jasmine season in the market is between August and January whereas Chrysanthemums start arriving in July lasting up to October. However, due to Dussehra the demand for Chrysanthemum, Marigold and Aster upturns during October. Most of the trade takes place at predetermined contract prices. The city service buses carry maximum flowers to the city market from the villages and suburbs of Bangalore whereas long distance flowers are transported by bus, truck or train.

Ghazipur Phool Mandi, Delhi –

The Ghazipur flower market in Delhi is very vast, and it serves all types of flower needs to wholesalers, retailers, and final consumers. One can witness the true beauty of this market during midnight at around 3 am to enjoy the colourful flowers and its aroma to soothe your soul. The stalls are beautifully decorated with exotic flowers, and the famous flower basket is sold here at wholesale rates. One can easily find diverse species of flowers here. Artificial flowers in the shades of yellows, reds, oranges and whites are also available. The predominant flower species traded at this market are: Lilies, Orchids, Gerberas, Roses, Marigold, Anthuriums, Carnations, Tulips and Aster.

Dal Lake Flower Market, Srinagar –

The top-selling flowers in India comes from this market that makes it famous. The market comes alive at the crack of dawn when the sellers gather in their canoes to sell fresh flowers. It’s a sheer visual delight to witness the beauty of the breath-taking floating Flower Market that offers an eye-soothing image of flowers.

Mallik Ghat, Howrah Bridge, Calcutta –

Found near the Hooghly River, this market is just beneath the divine Howrah Bridge. It offers exquisite roses and diverse blossoms; however, nothing overpowers the beholder other than the orange and yellow marigolds which satisfies the festive as well as the wedding rituals requirement.

Dadar Phool Mandi, Mumbai –

If you wish to witness a heaven on earth, then you should probably visit this amazing flower market in Mumbai known as the Dadar Phool Mandi. The time frame of 4 am to 9 am is just perfect to pay a visit to this market. Undoubtedly the aroma of the magical flowers and its beauty will make you smile wide. One can find a wide variety of flowers here as there are more than 600 stalls here making it marvellous and one of the big flower markets in India.

Devraja Market, Mysore –

The Devraja Market is Mysore is massively spread across 3 acres of land. This market has an ancient history associated with it that dates back to the reign of Tipu Sulatan in the 18th century. It is named after erstwhile Maharaja Dodda Devaraja Wodeyar. Apart from fresh flowers the market also trades, fruits, vegetables, puja materials, etc. It is one of the tourist sites of Mysore.
